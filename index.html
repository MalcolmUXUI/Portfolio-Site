A few examples of real projects where I helped teams clarify the problem, align stakeholders, and deliver systems that scaled beyond individual screens.

BigIron Auctions Platform Modernization

Role: UX Strategy Lead | UX/UI Consultant (platform, systems, handoff)

Focus: Reduce build risk and cross-team ambiguity by making workflows, states, and constraints explicit before implementation.

Receipts:

  • Role-based feature mapping tied to Jira, so engineers could see who each screen served and what “done” meant.
  • Dev-ready interaction coverage, including happy paths, empty states, validation, permissions, and error handling.
  • Operational reality translated into UX clarity, aligning status messaging and expectations to backend rules.

Outputs: User flows, annotated prototypes, edge-case inventory, PRD notes, accessibility and responsive component guidance.

Outcome: Fewer implementation surprises and less rework because edge cases and constraints were surfaced early.

Cedar Bluff Technologies (SpecVision)

Role: Brand Strategy Lead | UX Consultant (positioning, systems, product narrative)

Focus: Made an AI-assisted product feel credible by grounding messaging, UX decisions, and scope in real dealership workflows.

Receipts:

  • Thin-slice MVP scope with in-scope vs out-of-scope decisions and clear decision points
  • Dealership-ready messaging framework, with plain-language rules that avoid inflated AI claims
  • Competitive positioning story that explains differentiation without sounding salesy
  • Brand and usage guidelines for both the product and field-facing materials

Scope: Partnered founder-direct to shape positioning and UX narrative from the ground up, keeping product, marketing, and pilot materials consistent.

Outcome: Reduced pilot confusion and shortened sales conversations by making the value easy to understand, repeat, and trust.

About Me

My background spans high-stakes projects in design, brand systems, and customer-facing operations. That combination taught me how to see the system, not just the interface.

I’m Mark Malcolm, a UX Consultant and Senior Designer who helps teams navigate complexity and turn it into systems they can actually build, use, and trust. I’m a product partner, not just a screen designer.

My work sits at the intersection of UX strategy, brand systems, and real-world delivery. I’ve spent my career inside environments where clarity matters: strict brand guidelines, multi-role workflows, high-stakes operations, and teams trying to move fast without breaking things. That background trained me to look beyond individual screens and focus on how decisions ripple across people, tools, and process.

I’m especially effective when problems feel messy or undefined. I turn complex workflows into clear, buildable systems, helping teams surface what’s really happening, align stakeholders around shared goals, and translate ambiguity into clear structure. That often means guiding conversations, tightening requirements, and designing systems that scale, not just interfaces that look good.

I’m comfortable moving between hands-on design and strategic leadership, depending on what the business needs. If there’s a common thread in my work, it’s this: I reduce ambiguity and rework, so delivery moves faster, and I’m at my best when helping teams find a thoughtful, buildable path forward.

How We’ll Tackle It

Before we design anything, we clarify the decision we’re actually making. I use a repeatable framework that keeps decisions grounded and reduces rework as we go, but I’m not precious about it. We’ll adjust to fit real constraints, timelines, and stakeholders.

  1. Align on the Real Decision

    We define the actual decision being made, who it affects, and what success means. This prevents solving the wrong problem elegantly.

    Output: shared problem framing, success criteria, constraints.

  2. Expose the Current Reality

    We map how the system actually works today, including handoffs, workarounds, and unspoken assumptions. We mark where confusion, repetition, or quiet friction show up.

    Output: workflow map, friction audit, structural gaps.

  3. Clarify the Path Forward

    We design a clearer structure with roles, steps, and guardrails so the next action is obvious at each point. The goal isn’t decoration. It’s decision clarity.

    Output: proposed flow, interaction structure, content logic, edge cases.

  4. De-Risk Before You Build

    We test the direction early using lightweight prototypes or structured reviews so engineering effort isn’t spent validating assumptions.

    Output: validated changes, resolved questions, committed direction.

  5. Ship, Observe, Tighten

    Once live, we watch for repeated questions, support load, or breakdowns in the flow. Then we refine the system so it strengthens over time instead of decaying.

    Output: iteration plan, tightened standards, updated documentation.
